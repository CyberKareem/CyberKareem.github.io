The test is almost split into two parts; external and internal. The initial external infrastructure is a lot of fun. Once you’re inside the network, it’s up to you to compromise the visible infrastructure in scope, escalate privileges and report on any vulnerability you find.</p><p>Overall, the exam environment was very stable, and I experienced no issues with connectivity or similar.</p><h2 id="content"><strong>Content</strong></h2><p>If you’re new to penetration testing, you’ll probably struggle with this exam. There is one task in particular that had me scratching my head for a while. I used a whiteboard for mind-mapping purposes throughout the exam.</p><p>During the external penetration test, you’ll encounter multiple vulnerabilities. In the end, I found three different ways to get a shell on the underlying system.</p><p>You’ll have to pivot to other domains and subnets and exploit the vulnerabilities present.</p><h2 id="reporting">Reporting</h2><p>My report was 81 pages long, with a lot of screenshots, executive summary, report structure, contents page, etc. eLearnSecurity is clear on the reporting requirements.
