Professional enquiries, assessments & disclosure

Start with the problem. Keep the first message simple.

For a professional opportunity, security assessment, collaboration, or responsible disclosure, choose the relevant path and share enough context to identify the right next step. Sensitive material can move to an agreed channel after the initial conversation.

Useful first-message context

  • Your name, organization, and role
  • The assessment track you are considering
  • A high-level asset type and approximate scope
  • Whether the environment is staging or production
  • The desired outcome, timing, and time zone
  • Who owns or can authorize the assets
  1. 01

    Fit check

    Confirm the risk surface, authorization owner, timing, and whether the requested work matches the available assessment track.

  2. 02

    Scope conversation

    Agree targets, access, exclusions, safety boundaries, communication, and the result your team needs.

  3. 03

    Written proposal

    Document the test plan, deliverables, schedule, retest terms, and commercial conditions before work begins.

Disclosure expectations · 03

Request the private channel before sending details.

  1. Confirm the target and authorization context.
  2. Share the minimum evidence needed to establish impact.
  3. Avoid real-user data, persistence, service disruption, and public exposure.
  4. Agree on a private channel and coordinated publication timeline.

Initiate disclosure safely

Use info@cyberkareem.com to request a suitable private disclosure channel. Ordinary email is not a confidential report intake. Technical evidence can follow through the agreed route after the channel and recipient are confirmed.

Do not include exploit code, credentials, personal data, or unpatched technical detail in the initial message.
